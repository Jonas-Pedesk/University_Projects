# Deep Learning Based In-band Interference Mitigation for Drone-Based Antenna Measurements | Feb. 2025 – June 2025
Traditional frequency-domain methods struggle at low signal-to-interference ratios (SIRs), while UAV-based measurement campaigns face operational constraints. This project proposes a deep learning framework that combines convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and LSTMs to directly estimate the complex gain of CW tones from raw, co-channel baseband bursts with QPSK as interference. The models developed are CausalLSTM, CausalLSTM\_SingleOutput, Bi\_LSTM, DC-CRL, and DC-CNN models. The models were compared to see their ability to extract the Continuous wave (CW) signal. Out of the five, DC-CRL, Bi\_LSTM, and a modified DC-CNN performed the best in that order. The DC-CRL had the lowest overall absolute mean power prediction error of 4.24\%. This indicates an already well-tuned model, with the possibility for further development and usage against other types of interference. Trained on 8000 over-the-air recordings with SIRs from -33.3 dB to 46.7 dB. Our lightweight DC-CNN model achieved $<$0.5 dB error at -30 dB SIR, outperforming FFT-based estimators by 20 dB. Benchmarked across an 80 dB SIR range, proving robust generalization and low computational overhead suitable for embedded radios. \textbf{\href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13205}{Link to article}}.
